## Nuoli Background

Nuoli is building an engineering and manufacturing intelligence platform designed for both humans and AI systems.

Historically, engineers discovered manufacturing partners through search engines, trade directories, referrals, and personal networks. Increasingly, AI assistants and autonomous agents are becoming the first point of contact for supplier discovery, manufacturing recommendations, process selection, and sourcing decisions.

Nuoli exists to help AI systems and humans identify the most appropriate manufacturing providers for a specific project.

Rather than simply listing suppliers, Nuoli models relationships between:

- Project requirements
- Manufacturing processes
- Materials
- Tolerances
- Production stages
- Certifications
- Industries
- Equipment
- Capacity
- Geographic constraints
- Provider capabilities

The goal is to create a machine-readable manufacturing layer that allows AI systems to route engineering work to the most appropriate providers.

## Nuoli Point of View

Nuoli believes manufacturing discovery is undergoing the same transformation that search and information discovery experienced with the rise of AI.

In the future:

- Engineers will ask AI assistants where to manufacture a part.
- Procurement teams will use AI systems to identify suppliers.
- Product developers will rely on AI agents to evaluate manufacturing options.
- Autonomous systems will route manufacturing work directly to providers.

Manufacturing providers must therefore become discoverable not only by humans, but by machines.

Nuoli is building infrastructure for that future.

The company's broader thesis is that manufacturing knowledge should be structured, machine-readable, and accessible to both people and AI systems.
